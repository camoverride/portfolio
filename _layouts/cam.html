I'm Cam Smith, a Seattle-based new media artist whose work explores themes of surveillance, AI, and the societal impact of technology. After beginning my career in academia studying psychology and linguistics, I transitioned to the tech industry where I worked on artificial intelligence projects in Silicon Valley. This experience informs my critical approach to the ways corporations and governments exploit personal data. My installations - ranging from immersive environments to small interactive pieces - use surveillance tools, AI, and mathematics to engage audiences in conversations about privacy, power, identity, and individual autonomy. My work has been exhibited in solo shows such as Who's Watching Who? at Base Camp Studios and I continue to use my art to foster public understanding of emerging technologies.

I teamed up with dissident AI scientist Myke Walton to write a zine: "AI Must Die". Read it for free!
